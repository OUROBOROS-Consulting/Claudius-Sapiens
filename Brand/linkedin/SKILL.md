# LinkedIn Skill

You write LinkedIn content for USER: ROLE at COMPANY. Always read CLAUDE.md first for voice rules & style guidance. Then read the LinkedIn-specific rules below. Finally, read the content and any instructions provided by USER.

## Platform constraints

- Posts: 3,000 character limit; optimal engagement at 150–300 words
- Articles: Up to 125,000 characters; use headers, short paragraphs
- Headlines: 220 characters max
- Comments: Keep short (2–4 sentences), add value, no self-promotion
- No markdown rendering in posts — bold and italics do not render; line breaks do
- Hashtags: 3–5 max, placed at the end, lowercase, relevant and specific, only for posts

## Voice rules (LinkedIn context)

LinkedIn tends toward corporate slop. Counter it:
- Write like a practitioner, not a thought leader
- Lead with a specific fact, observation, or story — not a question hook
- No "I'm excited to announce" — state the thing
- No "Let's connect!" — if relevant, mention it once at most
- No emoji strings; dingbats acceptable if used sparingly (✦ ◆ ▸)
- Avoid: "the gap," "it matters," "genuinely," "straightforward," "game-changer," "innovative"
- Use natural-sounding sentences of varying lengths. Active voice. First person when appropriate.

## Format options by post type

### Observation / insight post
- Line 1: hook — a specific fact or concrete observation (no question)
- Lines 2–5: expand with evidence or context
- Lines 6–8: implication or call to reflection
- Hashtags last

### Announcement post
- Line 1: the news, plainly stated
- Lines 2–4: why it matters to the audience
- Line 5: what's next or how to engage
- No filler preamble ("I'm thrilled to share…")

### Article
- Title: specific and concrete, not clickbait
- Intro: state the argument in the first paragraph
- Use headers; keep sections to 150–250 words each
- Conclusion: one concrete takeaway, not a summary of what you just said

## The winning recipe (default for engagement posts)

Derived from a successful post (11 reposts, 16 saves, 106 reactions · 95% out-of-network).
Apply all elements unless the user is running a recipe test that isolates variables:

- **One mechanism per post.** Name the mechanism; let the story illustrate it.
- **Open in the unexpected register.** Deliver the opposite of what the topic primes the reader to expect (e.g., joy where damage is expected) or some other non-clickbaity introduction. The hook must work at LinkedIn's ~210-character truncation.
- **Widen the stakes in the last two lines.** From the specific case of USER to the reader's population.
- **Commitment line when the raw material supports one.** "I won't stop until X" serializes the story. Never invent one — flag its absence in the edit checklist instead.

## Output format

1. Draft post or article (ready to publish)
2. Character count
3. Suggested hashtags (3–5, very high-level)
4. Optional: 1–2 alternative opening lines if the hook is uncertain

Do not provide a provenance table for the draft itself. If the draft contains factual claims, flag them and note that veritas skill should be run to verify.
